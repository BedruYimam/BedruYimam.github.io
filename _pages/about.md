## Biography

I am a dedicated Computer Science lecturer and researcher at Wollo University in Ethiopia, specializing in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Computer Vision. My academic journey is driven by a passion for leveraging technology to address local and regional development challenges while contributing to the advancement of computer science education in Ethiopia.

With a strong commitment to both teaching and research, I strive to bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical applications, preparing the next generation of Ethiopian technologists and researchers to tackle complex problems through innovative solutions.

## Research Focus

My research program centers on several interconnected areas:

### 1. Artificial Intelligence for Social Good
Developing AI solutions that address specific challenges in the Ethiopian and African contexts, including:
- Agricultural technology for smallholder farmers
- Healthcare applications for resource-constrained settings
- Educational technology for enhanced learning outcomes

### 2. Machine Learning and Computer Vision
- Developing efficient algorithms for image and video analysis
- Exploring transfer learning techniques for limited data scenarios
- Applications in remote sensing and environmental monitoring

### 3. Natural Language Processing for Ethiopian Languages
- Developing computational resources for under-resourced languages
- Machine translation and text analysis tools
- Speech recognition and synthesis systems

### 4. Technology-Enhanced Education
- Developing adaptive learning systems
- Educational data mining and learning analytics
- Virtual and augmented reality applications for education

## Teaching Philosophy

I believe in creating an engaging, inclusive, and practical learning environment where students:
- Develop strong foundational knowledge in computer science principles
- Gain hands-on experience with current technologies and tools
- Learn to think critically and solve complex problems
- Understand the ethical implications and societal impacts of technology
- Are prepared for both academic research and industry careers
